What actually happens during a loft insulation installation?

A proper installation starts with checking your existing insulation, airflow and loft condition. Once that’s assessed, insulation is either topped up or installed fresh across the loft floor to reach the correct depth, with attention given to edges, eaves and any awkward areas.

Do you always install new insulation over the top of old insulation?

Not always. If the existing insulation is dry, evenly laid and in good condition, it can often be topped up. If it’s damp, compressed or poorly installed, removing it first usually leads to better long-term performance.

How do you make sure insulation is installed evenly across the loft?

We work across the entire loft space methodically, including corners, eaves and around obstacles. Even coverage is key because gaps or thin areas reduce the effectiveness of the insulation.

Will you check ventilation before installing insulation?

Yes, airflow is a key part of the assessment. We make sure insulation doesn’t block ventilation paths, particularly around the eaves, so moisture can escape properly and the roof structure stays in good condition.

What problems do you usually find in lofts before installing insulation?

Common issues include low insulation depth, uneven coverage, blocked airflow at the eaves and insulation that’s been disturbed over time. In older properties, we often see outdated materials that no longer perform well.
