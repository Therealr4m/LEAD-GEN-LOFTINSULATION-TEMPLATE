What’s the difference between a proper installation and a quick top-up job

A proper installation considers airflow, even coverage and correct depth across the entire loft, whereas quick top-ups often miss key areas and don’t address underlying issues.

Will insulation help if certain rooms in my home feel colder than others?

In many cases, yes. Uneven or insufficient loft insulation can lead to heat escaping more quickly in certain areas, so improving it can help stabilise temperatures across the home.

How do you prevent insulation from being compressed over time?

We install insulation at the correct depth and avoid unnecessary pressure on the material, as compression reduces its effectiveness. Proper installation helps it maintain performance long-term.

Do you check for signs of previous poor installation work?

Yes, we often come across insulation that’s been unevenly laid, disturbed or installed without proper airflow consideration, and this is addressed before improving the insulation.

Do you install insulation differently around loft hatches and access points?

Yes, these areas need careful attention because they’re common points for heat loss. We make sure insulation is fitted neatly around the loft hatch and that it doesn’t interfere with access, while still maintaining consistent coverage across the rest of the loft.
