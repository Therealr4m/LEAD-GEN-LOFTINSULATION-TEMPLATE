How much loft insulation should my {{LOCATION}} home have?

Current UK guidance recommends around 270mm of loft insulation. Many homes still have much less than this, which allows heat to escape through the roof more easily. Bringing insulation up to the correct depth makes a noticeable difference to heat retention.

How long does loft insulation take to install?

Most standard loft insulation jobs can be completed within a day. Larger lofts or projects involving removal of old insulation may take longer depending on access and layout.

Will new loft insulation actually make a difference?

Yes, especially in properties where insulation is below modern standards. Properly installed insulation reduces heat loss through the roof, helping rooms stay warmer and reducing how hard heating systems need to work.

Do I need to remove old insulation before installing new insulation?

Not always. If the existing insulation is in good condition, it can often be topped up. However, if it’s damaged, damp or uneven, removing and replacing it may be the better long-term solution.

What type of loft insulation is best for my property?

This depends on the loft structure and available space. Mineral wool is commonly used for standard lofts, while rigid PIR boards are often used where headroom is limited.
