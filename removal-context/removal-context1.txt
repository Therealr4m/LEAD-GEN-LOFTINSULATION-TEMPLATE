When insulation loses its structure or holds moisture, it stops performing properly. Adding new insulation over the top might seem like a quick fix, but it can trap hidden issues underneath. Over time, those problems don’t disappear — they usually come back in a more expensive way if the root cause isn’t properly dealt with first.
In situations where insulation is holding moisture or has broken down, simply covering it up doesn’t fix the issue. It can actually seal in damp conditions beneath the surface, allowing problems to continue unnoticed. Proper removal gives a clean starting point so the loft can perform as it should long-term.
If insulation has been affected by damp, pests or long-term wear, layering new material over it can create more issues than it solves. What looks like an upgrade on the surface can leave underlying problems untouched, which often leads to reduced performance or future damage.
Once insulation becomes compressed or contaminated, it no longer does its job properly. Adding more insulation on top doesn’t restore its performance — it just builds over the issue. Removing the existing material allows the loft space to be corrected properly before moving forward.
