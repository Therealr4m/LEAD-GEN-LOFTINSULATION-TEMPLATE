Every {{LOCATION}} property is slightly different. Some homes have shallow ceiling joists that limit how much insulation depth can be added, while others contain historic insulation layers that have settled unevenly over time. Because of this, loft insulation top-ups are never approached with a one-size-fits-all method.
<br> During installation, insulation is carefully laid between and across the ceiling joists to increase depth gradually and consistently. Particular attention is given to areas around loft hatches, pipework and roof edges where insulation must be installed precisely to maintain airflow and avoid compressing the existing material.
