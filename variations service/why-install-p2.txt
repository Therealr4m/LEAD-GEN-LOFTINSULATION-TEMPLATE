When insulation is compressed, uneven or missing in certain areas, it allows heat to escape and reduces overall efficiency.

Incorrect installation can also block airflow at the eaves, which may lead to condensation and moisture issues over time.

If insulation isn’t fitted consistently across the loft floor, certain areas will lose heat faster than others.

Gaps between insulation rolls or boards can significantly reduce how effective the system is as a whole.

When insulation is installed without considering airflow, it can create long-term ventilation problems within the loft space.

Uneven insulation depth is a major issue, as it prevents the loft from retaining heat properly.

If insulation is simply placed rather than properly fitted, it won’t deliver the performance homeowners expect.

Poor fitting around joists, edges and tight areas often leaves parts of the loft uninsulated.

When installation is rushed, important details like coverage and spacing are often missed.
